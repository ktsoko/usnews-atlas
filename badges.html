Badge FAQs

  1. Which file should I use?
    • JPG files can be used for display on the web or presentations. It comes with a white background, so it should not be placed on top of colored backgrounds or imagery. These particular jpgs are not prepared for print. If you need a JPG for print, please contact the design team.
    • PNG files are saved with transparent backgrounds and are the ideal file type for placing logos on top of photographs or other colored backgrounds. PNG files are low resolution and should not be used for print.
    • EPS files are vector images and can be scaled to any size without quality loss. It is the ideal file type for print materials, but can not be used for display on the web. EPS files also benefit from a transparent background.
    • SVG files are vector images that are supported by web browsers. When possible, this should be the primary file type for logos displayed on our website. SVG files allow us to provide a high quality image on all screen sizes, including retina devices.
  2. How do I get updated badges for my product launch?
    • If you are aware of the design lead assigned to your product team, you may contact them directly to have your product badges updated. Please allow 5-7 business days for your request to be completed. If you are unsure about which designer you should contact, you can email the team at webdesign@usnews.com.
  3. I found an error. How do I get a correction made to this site?
    • Please email webdesign@usnews.com with a description of the error.
  4. I'm working on a new ranking product. How do I get badges made?
    • All new badges will follow the badge guidelines. If you need a badge created, speak with the design lead assigned to your product team and they will be able to help create a badge which adheres to company standards. If you are unsure about which designer you should contact, please email webdesign@usnews.com with your badge request.
  5. I opened an EPS file and the fonts aren't displaying properly. What should I do?
    • Often the badge text won't display properly if you don't have our fonts loaded on your computer. You can install the fonts listed below, or we can provide you with a new version of the EPS with "outlined fonts." Simply let the design lead assigned to your product team know that you are experiencing this issue, and they will update the files for you. If you are unsure about which designer to contact, please email webdesign@usnews.com.
  6. Which file should I send to the Tech team?
    • SVGs are the best file type to use on the public website. However, as we move away from badges with dates on the site, you shouldn't need to send the Tech team new badges every launch. If a developer is requesting a new badge file, please alert the design lead assigned to your product team and they can provide the proper assets.
  7. Which fonts are used in the U.S. News Badges?
    • Both Titling Gothic and Rockwell are used in the badge design. For more guidelines and specifics about typefaces used, please view the Badge Guidelines.
